About Me

I am a PhD student in East Asian Studies at NYU. My current research explores the intersection of biopolitics, medical humanities, and media theory in Maoist China during the Global Cold War.

I examine how grassroots medical and broadcasting infrastructures converged to form a low-tech cybernetic feedback loop, effectively treating the rural social body as a programmable system. My broader research interests include cyberspace and cyberculture in East Asian societies, as well as cultural memory and production in (post)socialist China.
